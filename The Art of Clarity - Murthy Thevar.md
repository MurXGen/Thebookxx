## The core principles in The Art of Clarity

Here are the ideas from **Murthy Thevar's** book that readers highlight most:

### 1. Simplify before you amplify
Clarity begins with subtraction. Before adding more information or effort, **Murthy Thevar** urges you to remove what's unnecessary — the fastest way to see a problem clearly.

### 2. Structure your thoughts
Confusion is usually a formatting problem, not an intelligence problem. **Murthy Thevar** shows how to give your thoughts a structure so decisions almost make themselves.

### 3. Use strategic pauses
Speed feels productive but often creates noise. The book makes the case for deliberate pauses that let clarity surface before you act.

### 4. Separate the problem from the story about the problem
Much of our stress comes from the mental chatter around a problem, not the problem itself. **Murthy Thevar** teaches you to isolate the real issue and quiet the rest.
